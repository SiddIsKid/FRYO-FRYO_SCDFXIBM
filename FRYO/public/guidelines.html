DURING THE WARNING:


1. Bring in outdoor possessions (lawn furniture, grills, trash cans) or tie them down securely.
2. Listen to your local radio or television station for updates.
3. If evacuation appears necessary, turn off all utilities at the main power switch and close the main gas valve.
4. Have immunization records handy (or know the year of your last tetanus shot)
5. Shut off your electricity at the breaker panel
6. Clear out gutters and drains so that water can flow freely.
7. Keep important documents in a water proof locker in the house, if available, or carry it with you when evacuating.
8. Make a family communication plan, if separated.
9. Keep your GPS on for further information on evacuation routes.
10. Raise your appliances/ electronic gadgets to a higher level - bed, cupboard etc.
11. Put a sandbag in the toilet bowls to prevent backflow of sewage.


POST FLOODING:


1. Stay away from flood water - do not attempt to swim, walk or drive through the area
2. Be aware of areas where water has receded. Roadways may have weakened and could collapse.
3. Avoid downed power lines and muddy waters where power lines may have fallen.
4. Throw away any food and bottled water that comes/may have come into contact with floodwater.
5. Come back home only when officials ask you to do so.


WHAT NOT TO DO:


1. Panic or hyperventilate.
2. Drive or use any vehicle through the flooded area.
3. Walk through running water, 6 inches of running water can knock one off their feet, instead use a stick to measure the depth of the water.
4. Touch electrical appliances with wet hands.
5. Carry non-essential objects like clothes, bags etc. while evacuating.
